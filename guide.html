What is Chess?

Chess is a two-player strategy game played on an 8x8 board. Each player commands an army of 16 pieces, each with unique movement patterns. You must use your pieces to control the board and outmaneuver your opponent.

Objective of Chess

The objective of chess is to checkmate your opponent's king. Checkmate occurs when the king is under attack and there are no legal moves available to escape, block the attack, or capture the threatening piece.

How a Game Ends

A game of chess can end in checkmate, stalemate, resignation, or a draw due to insufficient material or repetition of moves.

Castling

Castling allows the king to move two squares toward a rook, while the rook jumps to the square next to the king.
